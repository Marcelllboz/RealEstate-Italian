The SA sales process — end to end

Prospecting & appraisal

An agent's opinion of likely selling price — NOT a valuation (only a licensed valuer gives those).

Sales Agency Agreement

Signed before marketing. Sets commission, marketing costs, duration, and the vendor's acceptable selling price. Exclusive vs general agency. Advertised price can't sit below the prescribed minimum — SA's anti-underquoting rules.

Campaign

Photography, portals, signboard, opens. Method chosen: private treaty (advertised price, negotiated offers) or auction (no cooling-off).

Form 1 — Vendor's Statement

SA's disclosure document under the Land and Business (Sale and Conveyancing) Act 1994: title, encumbrances, easements, zoning, rates. Usually prepared by a conveyancer. For auction: available for inspection 3 consecutive business days before, and at the auction.

Offers / auction day

Private treaty: written offers on the contract. Auction: reserve set, limited announced vendor bids, fall of the hammer = unconditional.

Contract of sale

Conditions may include subject-to-finance, subject-to-sale, building & pest.

Cooling-off

Private treaty buyers: 2 clear business days. None at auction.

Conditional period

Finance approval, inspections. All conditions met = unconditional.

Settlement

Conveyancers transfer title through Land Services SA, typically 30–60 days after contract. Keys over, commission paid from proceeds.

Form 1 — what you must know

  • Required for every residential land/business sale in SA.
  • Contains title particulars, mortgages, encumbrances, easements, zoning, rates, government notices.
  • Must be served on the purchaser — cooling-off doesn't start until proper service.
  • A defective Form 1 means cooling-off rights may never expire → deals fall over. Respect the conveyancer.
  • Auction: on display 3 consecutive business days before + at the auction.

Cooling-off — the rules

  • 2 clear business days, running from service of Form 1 (or contract, whichever is later).
  • Buyer cancels in writing; forfeits at most a small holding deposit (max $100).
  • No cooling-off when: bought at auction (or same-day contract to highest bidder), buyer signed a legal-advice waiver certificate, or buyer is a company.

Week 2 deep work

Listing presentation outline

  • Connection — their story, why they're selling. Listen 80%.
  • Local proof — median, growth, DOM, 3 comps. Cold.
  • Price — honest CMA range, never buy the listing with fantasy numbers.
  • Method — auction vs private treaty for THIS home, with reasons.
  • Marketing — what, where, cost, why each item earns its money.
  • The agency & you — your energy + their track record.
  • Fees — clear, unflinching, tied to outcome.
  • Close — "Are you comfortable for me to get the paperwork started?"

The Italian downsizer conversation

  • Trust before transaction — 40+ years in that home. Slow down.
  • Family in the room — adult children often drive it; address everyone, defer to the parents.
  • Respect the home — the garden, the tomatoes, the cantina. Mean it.
  • Italian for warmth, English for contracts — legal matters must be understood in their strongest language. Compliance, not just courtesy.
  • Their fear — being rushed, tricked, losing dignity. Your answer: patience, transparency, written follow-ups.

Rules of engagement

  • Always Lei / voi (formal) with elders. "Tu" with a 75-year-old signora before she invites it = instant disrespect.
  • Italian for warmth; English for contracts. Never let a legal detail ride on beginner Italian.
  • Imperfect Italian with effort beats silence. They will correct you, feed you, and remember you.

The scripts.

A script you can't deliver cold isn't yours yet. Drill 15–20 minutes daily until each one sounds like you, not like a script. Smile while talking — it's audible.

01The 30-second introduction

Ten times out loud, then once recorded. 25–30 seconds, ending on the free-appraisal hook.

"Hi, I'm Marcello Barone — I'm a sales agent with [firm] working across [core suburbs]. I grew up in an Italian family, so I work a lot with families in the Italian community around Campbelltown and Payneham — especially people who've been in their home thirty, forty years and are starting to think about the next chapter. If you ever want to know what your home's worth in today's market, that conversation costs nothing."

02"You seem young" — own it

They're really asking: "Can I trust you with my biggest asset?" Answer THAT.

A · Hunger + backing
"You're right, I'm 19 — and that's exactly why I'll outwork every agent in this market for you. And you're not just getting me: you're getting [firm]'s whole team and [X] years of results behind every decision. I bring the energy, they bring the track record — you get both."
B · For the Italian family, with a smile
"Sì, sono giovane! But my nonna taught me two things — respect, and hard work. You'll get both, and you'll get my number answering every time you call. Try getting that from the others."
C · Proof-first
"Fair question. Ask me anything about this market — Campbelltown's median, what sold on your street, where prices moved this year. Judge me on what I know and how hard I work, not my birth year."
Never: apologize, get defensive, or over-explain. One confident answer, then move forward.

03Phone callback / prospecting

Every buyer call is a potential listing call — the gold is in the last question.

"Hi [name], it's Marcello from [firm]. Thanks for coming through [address] on Saturday — I wanted to ask what you thought of it?

[If not right:] "No problem — tell me what you're actually looking for, and I'll keep an eye out before things hit the portals."

[Always end:] "And just so I can point you right — do you have a place to sell first, or are you ready to buy?"

04Open-home greeting + qualifying

Three beats: buying or browsing → timeframe → home to sell. Conversational, never interrogation.

"Hi, welcome in — I'm Marcello. Have a wander, it's a beautiful home."

[Later, relaxed:] "So what brings you out today — buying for yourselves, or just keeping an eye on the market? … Do you have a home to sell as part of the move?"

05The appraisal ask

Fence-line, door, or phone. No pressure, pure value.

"I'm not here to push you to sell — most people I talk to aren't selling. But the market's moved a lot in [suburb] this year, and most owners are off by $50,000 or more on what their place is worth. A proper appraisal is free and takes twenty minutes. Worst case, you know your number."
